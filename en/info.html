Functioning of the French vote under the Fifth Republic

WHO ?

French adult citizens have the right to vote. To be able to vote, citizens must be able to enjoy their civil and political rights. you must also be registered on the electoral roll of the polling station (see where).

WHY ?

The instigators of the French democratic system considered that this way for the people to vote is the most accurate way to approach the definition of democracy. It is also a strategy that logistics on a national scale.

WHERE ?

Voting takes place in a polling station, which is a place that is set up for the occasion and is governed by a set of rules and regulations. by a precise legislation, the electoral code. The latter provides for the material organisation of the electoral process. publication of results. A prefectoral decree divides each commune into as many polling stations as necessary, in order to avoid exceed 1,000 voters per office. Each polling station must have a display area, one or more voting booths and a voting table. voting and counting. The poster area is designed to inform citizens about the modalities, Civic reminders... Booths are subject to rules, in fact, at least one booth is required for every 300 people. voters and at least one wheelchair-accessible one. Votes are placed in a ballot box of which at least 4 sides are transparent. The number of counting tables shall be less than or equal to the number of polling booths, and these The latter are arranged in such a way that citizens can move around them.

HOW ?

Citizens who meet the requirements can register and go to the polling station at the following times its opening hours. They may vote themselves or vote by proxy. This proxy is carried out when a citizen cannot vote on his own, but this absence must correspond to one of the following cases provided for by law. The citizen in question in this case chooses a citizen who will vote according to his/her will. After the counting of votes, the president of the polling station draws up the minutes and then proceeds to the proclamation of results. The results are displayed in the polling station.

WHEN ?

The voting situations are relatively numerous, we can mention among others the elections municipal, departmental, legislative and presidential elections and referendums for example (although it is not possible to infrequent). What all these events have in common is their political dimension, they are all related to the direct or indirect functioning of the French government.
